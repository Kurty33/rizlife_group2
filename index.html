El Filibusterismo

El Filibusterismo is a novel written by José Rizal and was published in 1891. This is considered a sequel to the previous novel titled Noli Me Tangere. The novel talks about the scenario of the Philippine society under the Spanish rule as Filipinos had to face intense social injustices.

This type of literature describes revolution, corruption, and excesses of power. Simoun is the hero of the novel, who also happens to be a rich mysterious jeweler. He comes to the Philippines and has ambitions for a revolution in the country.

The novel condemns societal structures, the Church, and the government in describing the struggles that the Filipino people have faced during oppressive rule. El Filibusterismo is notable for having an intense, darker version of Noli Me Tangere and for issuing a call to Philippine society regarding changes and reform.
